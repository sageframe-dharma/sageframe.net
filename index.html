Andrew T. Marcus // Tyro Sageframe

Sageframe is a personal knowledge architecture — a system of systems where infrastructure, documentation, philosophy, and creative practice are unified under a single living taxonomy. Built in Obsidian, deployed on self-hosted infrastructure, and governed by its own cartographic discipline, Sageframe treats the act of organizing knowledge as a practice in itself: part engineering, part epistemology, part meditation. It draws on Zen concepts of impermanence and beginner's mind to formalize how one person learns, builds, maintains, and reflects — then makes that process legible, reproducible, and open. If Borges had a homelab and Wittgenstein wrote YAML, they might have built something like this. Learn more about Sageframe as a knowledge architecture.

Tyro Sageframe is the persona I work under — "tyro" literally means beginner — because when you throw yourself headfirst into programming, systems thinking, AI, and informatics from absolute zero, you might as well own the bit and name yourself after the journey.

The software and projects here are a byproduct of that practice: explorations from love or curiosity and tools built out of genuine need, released because others probably need them too. I'm interested in digital sovereignty — keeping your files yours, your tools local, your thinking your own. Professional work and consulting at atmarcus.net.

Forteller in development
CLI tool for tending the relationship between a configuration vault and the machines it describes. A config file carries a pragma — a comment block declaring where it belongs and what should happen when it arrives. The file is both the artifact and the manifest. Push-only, vault-first, no agents, no YAML sprawl. Part of the Kṣetra-Ops suite.
